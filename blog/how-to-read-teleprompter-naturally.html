How to Read a Teleprompter Naturally (Without Looking Like a Robot)

5 min read • Updated Nov 2025

The "teleprompter stare" is the enemy of engagement. You know the look: eyes locked on a specific point, minimal blinking, and a monotonous delivery. Here is how to fix it.

1. The "Distance Rule"

The closer you are to the camera, the more obvious your eye movement becomes. If you are using a phone or laptop teleprompter, try to position yourself at least 3-5 feet away from the lens. This reduces the angle your eyes need to travel to read the text.

2. Use Voice-Activated Scrolling

Manual scrolling forces your brain to multitask: you are reading AND trying to control the speed. This kills your natural cadence.

Tools like VoicePrompter listen to your voice and scroll automatically. If you pause for emphasis, it pauses. If you speed up, it speeds up. This allows you to focus 100% on performance, not mechanics.

3. Write for the Ear, Not the Eye

We don't speak the way we write. When writing your script:

  • Use contractions (don't say "do not", say "don't").
  • Keep sentences short.
  • Add [PAUSE] markers in your script to remind yourself to breathe.

4. The "Active Line" Position

Always keep your active reading line in the top third of the screen, as close to the camera lens as possible. If you read from the bottom of the screen, it will look like you are looking down. VoicePrompter sets this active area correctly by default.

5. Blink!

It sounds simple, but people forget to blink when reading scrolling text. Make a conscious effort to blink at the end of sentences. It resets your gaze and makes you look human.
