## 2. CuePrompter - simplest, but no voice scrolling

**Best for: a quick, no-frills run-through at your desk.** CuePrompter is one of the oldest browser teleprompters, and its appeal is simplicity: paste text, set a speed, go full-screen, no signup. The trade-off is that it's **fixed-speed auto-scroll only** - no voice scrolling - and the interface shows its age, with no script saving and limited options. Fine for a fast rehearsal; not for keeping pace while you actually record.

## 3. Speakflow - polished, but voice scrolling is paywalled

**Best for: a clean web prompter if you don't need voice on the free tier.** Speakflow has a nice interface and offers voice-activated scrolling - but on the **free tier that voice scrolling is locked**, and there's a word-count limit. You're effectively getting manual/auto-scroll for free, with the headline feature behind a paid plan. Worth knowing before you build a workflow around it.

## 4. TeleprompterMirror - basic and local

**Best for: a simple free tool with optional sound-based scrolling.** TeleprompterMirror is a straightforward web prompter where you paste a script, set a speed, and play; in Chrome you can enable a microphone button for sound-activated scrolling. Scripts are stored locally in your browser. It works, but it's basic, and its voice option is sound-based rather than true word tracking.
