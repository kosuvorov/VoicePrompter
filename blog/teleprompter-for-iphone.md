# Teleprompter for iPhone That Scrolls as You Speak

Yes, your iPhone can run a teleprompter that follows your voice instead of scrolling at a fixed speed. [VoicePrompter for iOS](https://voiceprompter.app/ios/) listens as you talk, matches your words against your whole script, and scrolls at your pace - and it either records 4K video right in the app or floats your script over TikTok and Instagram while their camera records you. Here's how that works in practice.

## Why fixed-speed scrolling fails on a phone

Most iPhone teleprompter apps make you set a scroll speed before you hit record. But nobody speaks at a constant rate. You slow down for the important line, speed up through the setup, pause when a dog barks. With fixed scrolling you end up chasing the text or waiting for it, and the take sounds like what it is: reading.

I built VoicePrompter around the opposite idea - the script should follow you. The app tracks your speech against the entire script, not just the next few words. Skip a paragraph, ad-lib a joke, go back and redo a sentence: the text finds you and keeps scrolling. It even scrolls backward if you restart a line from earlier, which most prompters simply can't do.

One early iOS reviewer described it better than I could: *"Downloaded the app for the first time yesterday and in less than 5 minutes, I was all set up. The scroll by voice feature is super smooth and the interface is user friendly."* - Holdridge87, App Store review.

## How do you record a video with a teleprompter on iPhone?

You have two ways, depending on where the video needs to end up.

**Record inside the app.** VoicePrompter has a built-in camera: up to 4K, your choice of lens, even 10-bit HDR. The script scrolls just under the front camera, so your eyes stay close to the lens. This is the clean path for YouTube videos, course lessons, and talking-head clips you'll edit later.

**Float the script over another app.** For TikTok, Instagram Reels, or Snapchat you usually want to film in *their* camera to use their effects and posting flow. VoicePrompter opens a picture-in-picture window that stays on top of every other app. Open TikTok, start recording, and read from the floating script - the phone records you through the camera, not the screen, so the prompter never appears in your video. There's a full guide in [teleprompter for TikTok and Reels](teleprompter-for-tiktok-and-reels.html).

## Three scroll modes for different situations

Voice tracking is the headline, but it's not always the right tool, so the app has three modes:

- **Voice scrolling** - real word tracking. Best for scripted takes where you want a natural, conversational pace. Works in 60+ languages, and recognition runs on-device.
- **Sound scrolling** - scrolls while it hears you talking, pauses in silence. Handy in noisy rooms where word recognition would struggle.
- **Constant speed** - the classic mode, for when you want full manual control or you're reading someone else's delivery.

If you're curious how these methods compare across the whole teleprompter market, I broke that down in [the five ways a teleprompter can scroll](teleprompter-scrolling-methods.html).

## Getting the read to sound natural

A prompter removes the memorizing; it doesn't automatically remove the "reading voice." Two habits fix most of it: keep the script window as close to the camera as possible (small eye distance means no visible eye drift), and write the script the way you actually talk - contractions, short sentences, the occasional aside. I collected the rest in [how to read a script without looking like you're reading](how-to-read-script-without-looking-like-reading.html).

Rehearsing matters less than you'd think. Because the scroll follows you, your first real take is usually usable - that's the difference between reading *with* the prompter and performing *for* it.
