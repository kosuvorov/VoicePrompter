Frequently asked questions

What is a voice-scrolling teleprompter?
A teleprompter that uses speech recognition to follow your actual words and advance the script as you speak - pausing when you pause - instead of crawling at a fixed speed. The full breakdown is in the five ways a teleprompter can scroll.
Can I use a teleprompter with Zoom or Google Meet?
Yes - on a Mac, VoicePrompter floats above the call and stays invisible during screen sharing. Step-by-step guides: Zoom and Google Meet.
Is there a free teleprompter app?
Yes - the VoicePrompter web app is completely free in any browser, and the native apps have a free tier with full voice tracking. See what's actually free on iPhone and the no-download online version.
What's the best teleprompter app?
It depends on your device and how the app scrolls - that's the axis that decides whether your read sounds natural. The cross-platform ranking is in the top 10 teleprompter software of 2026, with deeper dives for Mac and iPhone and iPad.
How do I read a script without looking like I'm reading?
Write like you speak, put the text as close to the lens as possible, and use voice-paced scrolling so the prompter follows your natural rhythm. The techniques are in how to read a script without looking like you're reading and how to use a teleprompter naturally.
Can I turn an iPad or Android tablet into a teleprompter?
Yes - a tablet is one of the best prompter screens you can get. Three iPad setups (tripod, beam-splitter rig, across the room) are in how to turn an iPad into a teleprompter; for Android tablets see the free browser teleprompter for Android.
Do I need a remote to control a teleprompter?
Not if it tracks your voice - the script advances as you speak, waits when you pause, and rewinds when you restart a line, which covers everything a remote does hands-free. See how to scroll a teleprompter without a remote.
