## The three tricks hiding inside "free"

I've tested the App Store's teleprompter category extensively, and "free" almost always means one of three things:

1. **Free app, paywalled core.** The download costs nothing, but voice scrolling, recording, or scripts beyond a demo need a subscription within minutes.
2. **Free with a watermark.** You can record, but the app stamps its logo on your footage - fine for practice, unusable for publishing.
3. **Free but fixed-speed.** The genuinely free apps are usually the simplest ones: a motorized crawl you have to chase, which is the main thing worth paying to escape. I explain why in [the five ways a teleprompter can scroll](teleprompter-scrolling-methods.html).

None of this is scandalous - developers have to eat - but it means "free teleprompter" searches mostly return apps that stop being free at the exact moment you try to use them properly.
