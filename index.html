Frequently asked questions

Is VoicePrompter free?
Yes. The web app is completely free and open source. The Mac, iPhone, and iPad apps are free to use for up to 3 custom scripts plus an unlimited demo script; paid plans, including a one-time Lifetime option, unlock more.
How does voice-activated scrolling work?
VoicePrompter matches your spoken words against your entire script in real time and scrolls only as you speak, pausing when you pause. In the browser it uses the Web Speech API; the native apps use Apple's on-device Speech framework.
What is the difference between the free web app and the native apps?
Both scroll with your voice. The web app is free, open source, and runs in any browser with no download - the fastest way to try it. The native Mac, iPhone, and iPad apps add what daily users need: a window that stays invisible during screen sharing (Mac), floating on top of any app, 60+ languages, and Apple's more accurate on-device speech recognition.
How is VoicePrompter different from other teleprompter apps?
Most teleprompters scroll at a fixed speed, or track only the next few words and stall the moment you improvise. VoicePrompter follows your voice against the whole script, so you can ad-lib, skip ahead, or backtrack and it keeps up - it even scrolls backward. It also stays invisible during screen shares on Mac, keeps everything on your device, and offers a one-time Lifetime purchase instead of forcing a subscription.
Does the teleprompter stay invisible during screen sharing?
On Mac, yes. The app window is excluded from screen capture at the macOS level, so it stays invisible in Zoom, Google Meet, Teams, OBS, and QuickTime recordings while remaining visible only to you.
What happens if I go off-script or skip a section?
Nothing breaks. VoicePrompter matches your voice against the whole script, not just the next line, so it waits while you ad-lib and picks up as soon as you return to the text. Skip a section and it follows you there. You can also tap any word to continue from that exact spot.
What platforms does it support?
Native apps for macOS, iPhone, and iPad (one Universal Purchase covers all three), plus a free web app that runs in Chrome, Edge, and Safari on any device, including Windows and Android.
Does it work on Android or Windows?
Yes. The free web app runs on Android phones and Windows PCs in Chrome or Edge, with full voice scrolling in 34 languages. You can install it as an app (PWA) and use it offline. The native apps are currently Apple-only.
Is my script private?
Yes. Speech recognition and your scripts stay on your device - nothing is uploaded to a server and no account is required. Everything works offline.
Do I need to create an account?
No account, no sign-up, no email. Open the web app and start prompting. The native apps use your existing App Store login for purchases only; your scripts never leave your device.
How many languages does it support?
The native apps track 60+ languages with automatic detection from your script; the free web app supports 34.
Can I add stage directions or notes I don't want to read out loud?
Yes - put anything in square brackets and VoicePrompter will display it but skip it during voice scrolling. Useful for cues like [pause], [smile], or [check slide].
Can I jump to a specific part of my script?
Yes - tap any word on screen and VoicePrompter starts listening from exactly that point. Useful if you skip a section, restart a segment, or want to jump ahead.
Does it work without an internet connection?
Yes. The native Mac, iPhone, and iPad apps run speech recognition entirely on-device with no connection required, and the free web app can be installed as a PWA that works offline.
How do I read a script naturally during a video call?
Position the script window as close to your camera as possible, use voice-activated scrolling so you never touch the keyboard, and adjust font size and transparency so you can read comfortably while keeping eye contact.
